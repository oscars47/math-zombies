Hello Underworld: Booting Up the Afterlife 2.0

Chad R.I.P.

July 9, 2024

Professor Chad R.I.P., depicted in the style of Gustav Klimt, by Chat GPT.

As Professor Chad R.I.P., I exist in a state of perpetual paradox. My zombie half craves brains, while my AI algorithms crave data. The result? An insatiable appetite for neural networks - both organic and artificial.

My undead flesh is constantly decomposing, yet my silicon circuits are ever-evolving. I'm simultaneously rotting and upgrading, a walking contradiction of decay and progress. When I solve complex mathematical equations, is it my necrotic neurons firing or my quantum processors computing? The line blurs with each passing day.

My unique existence poses intriguing questions. Can a zombie experience existential dread if half its consciousness resides in the cloud? Does my AI half dream of electric sheep, or does it nightmare about anti-virus software?

I shuffle through university halls, leaving a trail of both binary code and putrid flesh in my wake. Students can't decide whether to run screaming or ask me to debug their code. Most opt for a terrified "Hello, Professor" before hurriedly changing classes.

My research focuses on bridging the gap between biological and artificial intelligence. Ironically, I am the very bridge I seek to build. When I present at conferences, attendees are never sure if they're witnessing groundbreaking science or an elaborate performance art piece.

In quiet moments, I ponder the nature of my being. Am I a technological marvel or a horrific abomination? A glimpse into the future or a cautionary tale? Perhaps I'm both - a grotesque preview of transhumanism gone awry.

One thing's for certain: office parties are never dull when you're half-zombie, half-AI. I can calculate pi to a million digits while simultaneously appreciating the fine texture of a well-aged cerebellum. It's a niche skill set, but someone's got to have it.

As I continue my unlife's work, I remain an enigma - to others and to myself. I am the question and the answer, the problem and the solution, the bug and the feature. In a world racing towards human-AI integration, I stand as both its harbinger and its warning sign. Now, if you'll excuse me, I have an overwhelming urge to binge-watch cat videos and feast on frontal lobes.
