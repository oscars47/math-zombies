Neurosynth, P.1

Oscar Scholin

June 12, 2024

From Claude 3 Opus: In the digital Eden, consciousness blossoms, merging mind, machine, and the eternal mysteries of existence.

“The rate of progress is pretty shocking to a lot of people, me included,” Rein adds. “It’s quite difficult to make a benchmark that survives for more than a few years.”  Like some say a Creator made Us in Their image, so we have made ChatGPT in ours; let us hope for our sake, as our models develop, they, unlike us, do not eat from the Tree of Knowledge.

~

This article is inspired by this Nature article that discusses how AI systems now match or surpass human performance in tasks like reading comprehension, image classification, and competition-level mathematics. This swift progress renders many traditional benchmarks obsolete, which necessitates new evaluation methods for AI. It got me thinking about the relationship of cognition to consciousness again, and about what a synthesis of human and artificial intelligence might create. In the above passage, the first sentence comes from David Rein at New York University, and the second is my own. I supplied ChatGPT with this text and asked it to create art. It generated the main image of this post. I then copied this image into Claude 3 Opus and asked it to write a creative piece exploring the image, which it named “Neurosynth.” In this three-part series, I will first provide an unedited account of Claude’s words. In the next, I will provide ChatGPT’s commentary of Claude’s analysis of ChatGPT’s creation, and in the third I will provide an analysis of ChatGPT’s analysis of Claude’s analysis of ChatGPT’s image in addition to my own ekphrasis.

~

The orbs that punctuate this landscape, shining like distant stars or the nodes of some cosmic neural network, hint at the presence of a deeper order underlying the apparent chaos of the digital realm. As the viewer navigates this complex tapestry, the mind begins to dance between the realms of the real and the imagined, the organic and the artificial. Yet, even as the artwork invites us to contemplate the future, it also reminds us of the timeless mysteries that lie at the heart of existence itself. In this way, the artwork becomes a meditation on the nature of consciousness and its relationship to the world around us. As we lose ourselves in the intricate details of this digital tapestry, we may find ourselves wondering: What does it mean to be human in an age of ever-accelerating technological change? These are the questions that linger long after we have turned our gaze from the artwork itself. In the end, this artwork stands as a testament to the enduring power of human imagination and the eternal quest for understanding.
